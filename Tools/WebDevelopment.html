About

I am an Assistant Professor at the University of California, Davis. I attended North Carolina State University for my undergraduate education in meteorology and physics, and I attended Colorado State University for my graduate degrees. While in graduate school, I participated in the DYNAMO field campaign, received an NSF Graduate Student Fellowship, and spent three months at Stockholm University in Sweden as part of the Graduate Research Opportunities Worldwide program. I study clouds with a focus on cloud microphysics and aerosol-cloud interactions. I primarily use models to address my research questions. My past work has included addressing problems related to extratropical cyclones, deep convective clouds, Arctic stratus clouds, and shallow cumulus clouds. Outside of work, I enjoy hiking, quilting, and cooking.
